Personal Interests and Other Things

Trying to learn new skills can be surprisingly challenging especially when you're surrounded by tons of talented people posting their high-quality work online for the world to see or sharing stories how good they are in a specific thing. Right now I'm currently trying to learn how to draw, specifically Digital Art but the struggle in adjusting to how a drawing tablet feels as well as figuring out how to navigate these drawing softwares can be very intimidating especially when I feel like I feel the pressure to get good at it quickly when I'm going to college exactly for this reason. I know this is the wrong mindset to have but habits are hard to break especially the bad ones.

Other than drawing I originally tried my hand at writing which I quickly decided I wanted to keep as a hobby I'm also passionate about games not just playing them but understanding game design as well as since I find the thought process of making something interactive that is meant to be enjoyed fascinating in how deceptively difficult it can be. I hope I can eventually find the time and get the skillset to design a game of my own someday but I guess that's why I'm taking this course to begin with huh?


Media Literacy on the Decline?

Who knew I could find an outlet to talk about this in school for all things but I defintiely think people have become worse and worse in understanding media literacy. The whole "show don't tell" thing is a buzzword people spew out as a gotcha! to what they think is bad writting but the same people couldn't find subtext and subtlety until it was spelled out to them in bold letters and shouted out to the rooftops multiple times and they'd STILL have it fly over their heads.
